 Types of Depression

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), which is characterised by a depressed mood, Lack of interest in activities normally enjoyed, Changes in weight, Changes in sleep, Fatigue, Feelings of worthlessness and guilt for more than two weeks.
Persistent Depressive Disorder, which is chronic depression present for more days than not for at least two years. It can be mild, moderate, or severe.
Bipolar Disorder, which is characterized by periods of abnormally elevated mood known as mania.
Postpartum Depression: Depression can have its onset during pregnancy or following the birth of a child.
Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD): Premenstrual syndrome (PMS) could result in irritability, fatigue, anxiety, moodiness, bloating, increased appetite, food cravings, aches, and breast tenderness.
Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD): It could be characterised by experiencing depression, sleepiness, and weight gain during the winter months but feeling perfectly fine in spring
Atypical Depression: It is characterised by experiencing signs of depression but then suddenly perking up in face of a positive event.

 Symptoms of Clinical Depression

Low Mood
Decreased Interest or Pleasure
Changes in Appetite
Sleep Disturbances
Psychomotor Agitation or Retardation
Fatigue
Feelings of Worthlessness or Guilt
Difficulty Concentrating
Recurrent Thoughts of Death
